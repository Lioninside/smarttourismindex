Smart Tourism Index

A data based ranking of Swiss places as travel bases

Smart Tourism Index looks at destinations as travel bases, not only as end points. The main question is not how well known a place is. It is how a place works locally (Base Score), and what it opens up beyond itself (Access Score).

The index brings together public data on local quality, reach, landscape, cultural context, and tourism pressure to compare places through one clear lens.

This makes it possible to spot not only strong travel bases, but also places that may deserve more attention in a tourism system shaped by concentration and overtourism.

The Score

Two layers. One ranking.

The total score brings together two ideas.

Base Score looks at the quality of the place itself as a place to stay, move around, and spend time in.

Access Score looks at what the place opens up beyond itself.

That combination is the core of the project. A place can be strong because it works well on its own. It can also be strong because it connects well to bigger experiences around it.

The score is not black and white. It is a structured way to compare places through one clear lens.

What It Is

A different lens on destinations

Most travel platforms are built around attention.

Reviews. Listings. Popularity. Price. Visibility.

Smart Tourism Index looks at something else. It asks how useful a place is as a base, and how much it unlocks beyond itself.

That makes it useful in a different way. Not as a replacement for travel platforms. More as an added layer for interpretation.

This is not a booking tool. It is not a review platform. It is not a verdict on whether a place is good or bad.

How It Was Built

Public data. Deep integration work.

The hard part was not finding public data.

The hard part was making different public datasets work together in a way that is clean, comparable, and useful.

The project brings together public sources that were built for different purposes, different geographies, and different systems. That meant a lot of mapping, cleaning, alignment, and judgment.

So this is not a few APIs stitched together. The real work was in combining public data layers so they actually support each other.

That matters because a ranking is only as good as the way the underlying data has been made compatible.

How To Read It

Why places score differently

A place does not rank high because it is famous.

It ranks high if it works well as a base and gives strong access beyond itself.

That is why some globally known places end up lower than expected. High tourism pressure can drag a place down. A less obvious place can rise because it offers a better balance of local quality and reach.

This is part of the point. The ranking is meant to open a different conversation.
