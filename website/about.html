About SmartTourismIndex

SmartTourismIndex is an independent, data-driven project. It evaluates 193 Swiss destinations as bases for exploration — scoring each by access potential, base quality, practical comfort, and visitor pressure.

The goal is to make smarter travel choices visible and to support a more balanced distribution of tourism demand across Switzerland.

The index is built entirely on publicly available Swiss federal and geospatial data. It has no affiliation with any tourism board, hospitality group, or government body.

The Problem

Overtourism is a distribution problem.

Switzerland's most famous destinations — Zermatt, Lucerne, Interlaken — absorb a disproportionate share of visitor demand. The result is overcrowding, environmental pressure, and a diminished experience for tourists who followed the recommendation of an algorithm or a travel magazine.

The problem is not too many tourists. It is that too many tourists go to the same places. This is a data problem. And data can help solve it.

SmartTourismIndex was built to surface the alternatives — locations with equal or greater access potential, lower visitor pressure, and real capacity to host demand. Not hidden in the sense of remote or difficult, but overlooked in the sense of underrepresented in conventional recommendation systems.

Methodology

How STI measures destination value.

STI calculates a score for each of the 193 Swiss locations based on four dimensions. Each dimension is weighted and derived from real data rather than editorial judgment.

35%

Access

What can be reached from a location within one hour by public transport. This includes nature, lakes, hiking networks, cultural sites, scenic transport, and local hospitality — evaluated by both range and diversity.

25%

Base

How suitable the location is as a place to stay. Heritage, walkability, local restaurants, and proximity to water are used as objective indicators of overall base quality.

20%

Comfort

How easy it is to stay and move around without a car. Public transport frequency, summer climate, and accommodation availability define the practical conditions of a stay.

20%

OT↓

How saturated a location already is with visitors. High quality combined with lower visitor pressure represents a strategic advantage for more balanced travel.

Data Sources

Built on Swiss public data.

Every indicator in the index is derived from publicly available, official Swiss data. No editorial scoring, no surveys, no opinion.

Swiss Federal Statistical Office (BFS)

Tourism arrivals, overnight stays, accommodation supply, occupancy rates, and domestic/international visitor split — the primary source for tourism pressure indicators.

MeteoSwiss

Summer climate normals — average temperature, precipitation, and sunshine hours for June, July, and August. Used to assess seasonal comfort for visitors.

Swiss GTFS

The national public transport feed — stop locations, route frequency, and connection richness. Used to score PT strength and anchor stop quality per destination.

swissTLM3D

The national topographic landscape model — hiking trail networks, water features, and terrain data. Used to assess trail access and natural amenity within a 15 km radius.

OpenStreetMap

Points of interest including museums and restaurants within a 2 km radius of each location. Used to assess base quality and local cultural presence.

UNESCO

World Heritage Site designations as an input to heritage and cultural asset scoring within the reachable range of each destination.

Who built this

An independent project.

SmartTourismIndex was created by Clemens Bartlome, a Swiss tourism professional with more than fifteen years of experience across destination management, international sales, and digital strategy. He currently leads global FIT sales at Switzerland Travel Centre and previously worked as Country Manager in London.

SmartTourismIndex is an independent side project, developed in his own time and shaped by a strong interest in how data can support smarter travel decisions and a more balanced distribution of tourism value.
