About this project

Smart Tourism Index is an independent project. It started as a question: can public data offer a more neutral, structured view of destination value in Switzerland?

Why I Built It

The question behind the project

The idea came out of years working in Swiss tourism — watching how demand concentrates around the same well-known destinations, and wondering whether that was inevitable or partly a problem of information.

Existing platforms answer different questions. They are built for discovery, bookings, reviews. None of them are structured around the question of which places work best as a base — how well a destination functions locally, and what it gives access to around it.

That gap felt worth addressing. Not to replace anything, but to add a perspective that was missing.

Background

Professional context

My background is in Swiss tourism, international sales, strategy, and business transformation.

That includes leadership roles at Graubünden Ferien and Switzerland Travel Centre, as well as broader work across growth, product, and organisational change.

Smart Tourism Index is entirely separate from my day-to-day professional work. It is an independent side project, built outside of any current role or organisation.

Open Source

Open and independent

The project is open source. The full data pipeline, scoring logic, and source data references are available on GitHub.

That transparency is intentional. A ranking is only as credible as the decisions behind it — and those decisions should be visible and challengeable.

The index is not connected to any tourism board, government body, or commercial platform.
