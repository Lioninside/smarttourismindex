Methodology

How the score works

Smart Tourism Index does not try to answer every travel question. It focuses on one specific one. How strong is a place as a base for exploring Switzerland.

That means the score is not a full judgment of a place. It is a structured comparison through one defined lens.

Score Structure

Base and Access

The total score combines two layers.

Base looks at the local side of a place. This includes signals around local quality, environment, and how the place works on its own.

Access looks beyond the place itself. It measures how much value a place gives access to within reach.

That split is the core idea of the project. A destination can be strong because it is strong locally. It can also be strong because it opens up a lot around it. The most interesting places often do both.

Inputs

What the score is based on

The score is built from multiple public data signals.

These include local and regional context, tourism pressure, access, environmental factors, and cultural or structural indicators.

Not every signal matters in the same way. Not every place is strong for the same reason.

The goal is not to reduce destinations to one simple label. The goal is to create a more comparable view of place value.

Data Work

Why public data needed more than collection

The project uses public data, but that does not mean the process was simple.

The source data comes from different systems and different structures. It had to be cleaned, mapped, aligned, and interpreted before it could support a useful comparison.

That part was a major part of the work. Not because the data was hidden. Because making public datasets comparable is harder than it sounds.

Reading Results

How to read the ranking

The score should be read as a comparative signal.

A high score does not mean a place is better in every sense. A low score does not mean a place is weak in every sense.

It means that under this model, and for this specific question, a place performs differently.

That is also why famous places do not always rank highest. Visibility and suitability are not the same thing.

Limits

Limits

No ranking captures the full reality of travel.

Smart Tourism Index is one perspective. It is based on public data and explicit logic, but it still depends on definitions, source quality, and interpretation.

That is why the platform is best used as an additional lens. Not as a final answer.
